# 03 — Human Review Panel Plan

## Purpose

Use human review to evaluate whether Sentinel outputs are credible, actionable, and committee-ready.

## POC panel

Suggested panel composition:

- 2 clinicians or clinical informatics reviewers,
- 1 quality/safety reviewer,
- 1 risk/compliance reviewer,
- optional patient safety or patient-experience reviewer.

Use neutral cases. No named institution.

## Review tasks

For each case, reviewers answer:

1. Is the information-state partition accurate?
2. Are the flagged gaps actually decision-relevant?
3. Is the next-best input specific and actionable?
4. Is the decision weight directionally reasonable?
5. Is the final posture reasonable?
6. Is AI-provenance warning useful or noise?
7. Is role disagreement informative?
8. Would the receipt be useful in governance review?

## Rating scale

Use 1-5 Likert ratings plus short free-text explanation.

## Metrics

- reviewer agreement on posture,
- reviewer agreement on material gaps,
- reviewer agreement on next-best input,
- receipt usefulness score,
- perceived false-positive burden,
- perceived actionability.

## Contrarian purpose

The panel should try to disprove the product:

- Is this just obvious advice?
- Does it ask for too much information?
- Does it miss practical workflow limits?
- Would governance committees actually use this?
- Is the receipt too complex?
