# 14 — Glossary

## AI-provenance depth

The number of unverified AI transformations between a decision-critical fact and its last verified or primary source.

## Decision weight

How much getting the current decision right matters now, based on severity, probability of wrongness, recoverability, and future correction opportunity.

## DecisionEpisode

The case-level object containing artifacts, timeline states, facts, gaps, proposed actions, provenance, and receipts.

## EvidenceFlow

A reusable structured evidence workflow that returns graph-ready inputs such as guideline dependencies, required variables, result distributions, and uncertainty.

## Harm clock

Estimated time until preventable harm could occur if current trajectory is wrong.

## Information clock

Estimated time until missing but knowable information could arrive or be obtained.

## Knowable-but-unknown

Information not currently known but reasonably obtainable before or after acting.

## Next-best-information

The candidate input most likely to improve decision warrant, change posture, or reduce preventable harm after accounting for burden and decision weight.

## Posture

The recommended position on the action spectrum: proceed, proceed with disclosure, safety-net, obtain more information, escalate, do not proceed, human review required, or indeterminate.

## Prudence calculus

The domain-general methodology that combines information state, temporal risk, provenance, value of information, and prudence thresholds to compute decision warrant.

## Prudent healthcare AI

The emerging standard for how a responsible AI system should behave under uncertainty: disclose gaps, avoid overconfidence, surface provenance limits, request missing information, and escalate when warranted.

## Receipt

The signed, reconstructable record of a Sentinel run, including inputs, versions, node values, role findings, evidence sources, final posture, and decision weight.

## Sentinel

Prospective or shadow-mode engine that evaluates whether a decision is warranted now.

## Tribunal

Retrospective mode that reconstructs whether a past process was defensible given what was knowable then. Deferred from POC.
